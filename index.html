about

The shape of the work

I lead engineering, AI, and innovation at Arena, a digital advertising firm with deep political-cycle exposure and an unusually broad surface area (DSPs, social, CTV, direct mail). The role was created on purpose: a distinct vector for engineering excellence next to traditional agency work.

I'm most useful at the intersection of three things — production AI systems that have to actually run, hard data and information-theoretic problems, and the human side of getting teams to adopt new machinery. I authored our enterprise AI Policy v2.0 (7 core principles, 16-category Universal Data Restrictions, vendor- assessment rubric, 24-hour incident protocol) and chair the internal AI and Automation committees.

Outside work I build embedded electronics, run statistical tests on PRNGs because it's fun, contribute to local political campaigns, and keep a homelab in northern Utah running on more YAML than is strictly defensible.
